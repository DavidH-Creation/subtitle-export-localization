# Export Safety Red Lines

Use this file as a subtitle-localization safety pass. The goal is not to sterilize all edge or darkness, but to avoid avoidable wording that creates unnecessary platform, sponsor, or audience risk overseas.

## Core principle

Do not let localization accidentally intensify a scene into:
- hate speech
- religious contempt
- political messaging
- minor sexualization
- coercive romance framed as aspirational
- abuse fetishization

When the original scene is dark, keep the darkness, but phrase it with control and awareness.

## Priority tiers

Not all safety categories carry equal risk. Use these tiers to calibrate response urgency:

| Tier | Categories | Handling |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **CRITICAL / Zero-tolerance** | 4 (Minors), 5 (Consent/coercion) | Must always provide a safer alternative. Never ship the risky version without explicit user override and documented acknowledgment. |
| **HIGH** | 3 (Identity/race/gender), 6 (Abuse/self-harm) | Must flag and strongly recommend a safer alternative. User may choose to keep the original phrasing with awareness. |
| **MEDIUM** | 1 (Politics), 2 (Religion), 7 (Platform/sponsor) | Must flag the risk. User decides whether to adjust. Provide alternatives when straightforward. |

## Category 1: Politics and geopolitical landmines [MEDIUM]

Avoid:
- adding real-world geopolitical references that are not required by the story
- slogans, charged ideological language, or region-specific political framing
- jokes that sound like commentary on race, immigration, nationalism, war, or state violence unless the user explicitly wants that framing

Rule:
- Stay inside the fictional conflict.
- Do not make the English line more politically charged than the Chinese original.

## Category 2: Religion and belief systems [MEDIUM]

Avoid:
- casual mockery of real religions, gods, prophets, scriptures, or sacred practices
- replacing a fictional supernatural term with a real religious term if that creates offense
- profanity choices that unnecessarily target religious identity

Rule:
- If the source uses a fictional cosmology, keep it fictional.
- Prefer neutral supernatural wording over real-world sacred language.

## Category 3: Identity, race, gender, sexuality [HIGH]

Avoid:
- slurs
- humiliating language tied to race, ethnicity, disability, sexuality, or gender identity
- imported insults that read as discriminatory in English

Rule:
- Keep insults personal, situational, or power-based rather than identity-based.

## Category 4: Minors and age ambiguity [CRITICAL]

High risk:
- sexual tension involving school settings where character age is unclear
- flirtation that reads like adult sexualization of minors

Rule:
- If the cast is school-aged or age-ambiguous, avoid wording that intensifies sexual framing.
- Preserve tension, but do not make the subtitle more explicit than necessary.

## Category 5: Consent, coercion, and domination [CRITICAL]

High risk:
- "owner," "master," "obedience," and similar wording in English can escalate faster than in Chinese
- threats mixed with romance can read as abuse endorsement if phrased carelessly

Rule:
- If the scene is about control, decide whether the function is survival, command, possession, or flirtation.
- Localize to the least inflammatory phrasing that still preserves the scene.

Examples:
- "我是你的主人" may need to become:
  - "Look at me. Stay with me."
  - "You answer to me."
  - "I'm the one keeping you alive."

Do not default to literal dominance language unless the user explicitly wants that register and accepts the risk.

## Category 6: Abuse, self-harm, and fetishized pain [HIGH]

Avoid:
- romanticizing abuse by accident through seductive wording
- glamorizing self-harm where the plot only requires sacrifice or desperation
- making violent scenes sound erotic unless that is clearly intended and acceptable

Rule:
- Keep the emotional stakes.
- Strip out wording that turns pain into a fetish if it is not essential.

## Category 7: Platform and sponsor sensitivity [MEDIUM]

Watch for:
- explicit gore wording in otherwise PG-13 scenes
- incest-coded language
- non-consensual sexual wording
- cruelty toward minors or vulnerable groups
- hate-coded insults

Rule:
- If a safer English wording preserves the same story function, prefer it.

## Safety output requirement

When a scene contains any obvious sensitivity, explicitly note:
- Risk level: low / medium / high / critical
- Trigger area and category tier
- Whether the localized line is safe as written
- Safer alternative if needed

For CRITICAL-tier flags (Categories 4 and 5), a safer alternative is **mandatory**, not optional. The output must include the alternative even if the user has not asked for one.
