# Language Lanes

Use this file when the user wants localization beyond English or wants to compare language markets.

## Default lanes

### English

- Best for widest subtitle-led export testing
- Prefer clean, globally legible spoken lines
- Avoid translationese and over-the-top literal dominance language

### Spanish

- Keep emotional directness
- Allow slightly warmer or more expressive phrasing than English
- Avoid slang that is too country-specific unless the user wants LATAM-specific or Spain-specific wording

### Japanese

- Prioritize tone fit and character register
- Avoid over-literal transfer from Chinese phrasing
- Use this lane when the project is anime-coded or the user wants fandom-native delivery

### Korean

- Keep power dynamics legible and socially precise
- Works well for cold, sharp, status-heavy dialogue
- Avoid making everything sound like K-drama parody

### Thai

- Prioritize natural emotional readability
- Keep humor and teasing light-footed
- Avoid over-complication in subtitle timing

### Indonesian

- Prefer clear, contemporary spoken phrasing
- Keep lines compact and accessible
- Good for broad subtitle consumption and lighter tonal material

### Vietnamese

- Preserve speed and emotional clarity
- Avoid stiff formal constructions unless status is important
- Good for high-energy short-form dialogue

### Portuguese (Brazil)

- Allows expressive rhythm and emotional swing
- Keep it natural and contemporary
- Avoid slang overload unless the user explicitly wants high-local flavor

## Lane selection rule

If the user asks for multiple languages, keep the same source diagnosis and risk logic, but localize each lane according to its own natural rhythm and social tone.
