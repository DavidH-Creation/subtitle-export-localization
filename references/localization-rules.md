# Localization Rules

Use these rules when rewriting dialogue for subtitle-led export.

## Core principle

Translate function, not wording.

The English line should preserve:
- intent
- emotional force
- power dynamic
- scene utility

The English line should not preserve:
- Chinese sentence structure
- redundant emotional labeling
- culture-specific shorthand that will confuse viewers
- exaggerated phrasing that becomes cringe in English

## Common failure modes

### 1. Translationese

Symptoms:
- "You think you won?"
- "This matter is not over."
- "You dare?"

Fix:
- Rewrite into native spoken English.
- Choose shorter, sharper constructions.

### 2. Overheated melodrama

Symptoms:
- The Chinese line is fun on the page but too theatrical in English.
- Direct translation sounds campy in the wrong way.

Fix:
- Keep the energy, but lower the verbal temperature slightly.
- Let rhythm and implication carry the threat or flirtation.

### 3. Culture-locked slang

Symptoms:
- Net slang, fandom terms, or internet shorthand have no direct English equivalent.

Fix:
- Replace with an English-language social equivalent.
- If no equivalent exists, rewrite for scene effect rather than lexical match.

### 4. Exposition overload

Symptoms:
- The line explains more than an English subtitle should.

Fix:
- Cut to the actionable emotional point.
- Move detail into a separate line only if needed for comprehension.

## Subtitle mode defaults

- Prefer one short sentence over two medium ones.
- Avoid dense subordinate clauses.
- Let punchlines land fast.
- Remove filler words unless they define character voice.

## Character voice guardrails

- Cold strategist: clipped, precise, controlled
- Arrogant fighter: blunt, hot-blooded, contemptuous
- Rational enforcer: formal, exact, low-emotion
- Possessive lover: intimate, invasive, emotionally loaded
- Trickster or tease: playful, sharp, lightly ironic

## Localization test

Approve the line only if:
- a native English-speaking actor could say it naturally
- a subtitle reader could parse it instantly
- the line still sounds like that character
